Is HTTPS even beneficial?

I can't believe there are websites who still don't use HTTPS. I could see why there were concerns about HTTPS in the past, however it's 2018 now. HTTPS is very well supported at this point and any new website in development should be integrating HTTPS.

I've never thought about HTTPS slowing down a website, but I wouldn't mind if that were the case. It's astonishing that people care for such small performance gains instead of security. I wish she would have gone more in depth about why some certifcates cost $$ and why others don't. For example, how could/would I know that the a website is using a free certificate vs a paid. Does the free certificate guarantee the same level of security as a paid one?

I'm not even sure why there is any concern about attacks constantly on the rise against HTTPS. This could be said for just about any technological device in our current time.. As long as that technology is supported, there shouldn't be any concern of upcoming or existing threats as they are often dealt with quickly. As she mentioned, the best advice to combat new attacks is to keep your system up to date.

It was a great video on explaining why HTTPS is important and it's pros. Not only that but it also showed how arguments against it are incorrect..
